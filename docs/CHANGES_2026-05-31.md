# SIXsens — What changed (31 May 2026)

A short report on the latest round of improvements to SIXsens, written for anyone
on the team — not just engineers. Two things drove this update: making the
assistant **honest about what it knows**, and making the whole app **clearer and
nicer to use**.

---

## The headline fix: the assistant now knows when it *doesn't* know

**Before.** If you asked a question the knowledge base didn't really cover, the
assistant would honestly say *"I don't have a documented answer for this"* — but
the little confidence badge still showed a confident green **HIGH**, and it
pointed you to the wrong expert. The words and the badge disagreed, which makes
the tool hard to trust.

**Why it happened.** The assistant was rating its confidence on *how good the
sources it found were* (are they verified? recent? written by an expert?) rather
than on *whether those sources actually answer your question*. So a high-quality
document about a different topic would still make it look confident. It got worse
when you were sitting on a specific screen — the screen's topic would "bleed
into" the question and make unrelated documents look relevant.

**Now.** Confidence is measured by **how well the sources match your actual
question**, not by how polished those sources are. The result:

- Questions the knowledge base genuinely covers still come back **confident**.
- Questions outside it now correctly drop to **low confidence and escalate** —
  the assistant says *"no documented solution, let's ask the right person"* and
  routes you to the **right expert for that topic** (e.g. a settlement question
  goes to the settlement specialist, even if you happened to be on the Master
  Data screen at the time).

In short: the badge and the answer now tell the same story, and escalation
points to the person who can actually help.

---

## Knowledge Graph: now a real project map

The graph used to be a static, slightly empty diagram. It's now a **project
view** you can actually navigate:

- **Pick a project.** The title at the top is a dropdown — click it to switch
  between projects, or type to search for one. Three projects are included,
  inspired by real SIX initiatives:
  - **SDX – Digital Asset Tokenization**
  - **T+1 Settlement Migration**
  - **SFDR & ESG Data Services**
- **See progress at a glance.** Each project shows an **overall progress bar**.
- **Find yourself.** Your own department (Regulatory Affairs) is highlighted in
  **bright orange**, so you can immediately see where you sit relative to the
  other teams on the project.
- **See who's involved.** Click any department to slide open a panel listing the
  **1–4 people from that team working on the project**, with contact details.
- **It's readable.** Each project's diagram is laid out so the connecting lines
  **never cross over each other**, and a small **legend sits in the top-right**.

---

## Smaller refinements across the app

- **Employees directory** — clicking **"Contact"** now opens just the message
  window, instead of also popping open the full profile panel. Cleaner, less
  surprising.
- **Library** — removed visual clutter: the separate preview (eye) icon and the
  page-count column are gone. You still open a document's preview simply by
  clicking its row, and the download button stays.
- **Assistant answers** — removed the "Context used" strip from each answer, so
  the response is more focused on the answer itself.
- **History** — past conversations now display the assistant's full grounded
  answers correctly when you reopen them, not just plain text.

---

## What this means for the demo

- Ask an in-scope question (SFDR, FATCA, Master Data) → confident, sourced answer.
- Ask an out-of-scope question (e.g. an MT548 settlement edge case) → the
  assistant admits the gap, drops to low confidence, and routes you to the right
  expert. This "knows its limits" moment is now reliable and worth showing.
- The Knowledge Graph is a strong visual: switch projects, watch the progress
  bars, and point out your own department in orange.
